# PRL reviewer risk register

| risk | likely objection | current response | submission requirement |
|---|---|---|---|
| Novelty vs process tensors | "Restricted controls miss multitime structure" is folklore in the process-tensor literature. | The sharp content is exactness: complete marginal-channel tomography, a minimal Stinespring dilation, and a non-gauge direction with exact all-parameter agreement plus a first-order two-slot separator. The Letter positions this against process tensors, instrument-dependent Markov order, and single-probe Hamiltonian identifiability with a comparison table in the SM. | Keep the word "exact" tied to Proposition 1 only; never claim process-tensor tomography itself fails. |
| Curto-Fialkow terminology | "Flat extension" already has a fixed meaning in the moment problem. | The Letter explicitly disambiguates: here it is two-sided closure of the response-Hankel row/column space under registered shifts, unrelated to positivity or representing measures. | Retain the disambiguation paragraph verbatim. |
| Conditionality of the certificate | The order bound n_J is external input, so the certificate looks circular. | That conditionality is the theorem: the delayed-chain family proves unconditional self-certification is impossible; the certificate converts the external bound into an all-depth guarantee or returns INCONCLUSIVE. Ledger item O2 states it. | Never let "certified" appear without "for the declared order class". |
| Realization-theory priority | Ho-Kalman, HMM/PSR/WFA spectral learning, quantum realization, and especially QPI (Bennink-Lougovski, NJP 21, 083013 (2019)) already use block-Hankel reconstruction and the effective-dimension-bound-implies-completeness principle. | QPI is now cited head-on: it treats a fixed repeatedly applied channel with no intermediate interventions. This Letter treats a word-indexed response series generated by multi-letter interventions, lifted to values and first derivatives, with exact non-gauge blindness under complete marginal tomography, certificate states under perturbation, and a sealed device-referenced protocol. | Keep the QPI sentence in the related-work paragraph verbatim; never claim the finite-duration principle itself as new. |
| Hardware false positives | Two fez nulls crossed the gate, so the protocol failed on hardware. | Presented as the point, not a blemish: an unreferenced absolute threshold lost specificity, the sealed null confirmation localized a device-locked YX floor (pooled z = -6.20), and the preregistered device-referenced differential rerun scored 5/5 darks with 0/3 nulls (its window happened to be weak-floor, so no same-window A/B causal claim is made). Sensitivity transferred; absolute specificity did not - and the audit caught it. | Keep the fez failure and the sign drift (-6.20 to +4.77) in the Letter text; never present v3 as a silent fix. |
| Statistics discipline | Thresholds could have been tuned after unsealing. | Answer-key SHA-256 hashes published before unsealing; estimators frozen before scoring; per-family Bonferroni gates; figure normalizes each family by its own frozen gate and asserts consistency against stored verdicts in the generating script. | Ship the hashes and manifests in the artifact release; keep the in-script gate assertion. |
| Resource-claim scope | The O(m n_J^2) vs Theta(d^{4k}) comparison ignores shot noise and matched conditions. | The Letter counts certificate entries only and says so; the universal shot lower bound was withdrawn (R10), and ledger item O6 records the matched-order comparator caveat. | Do not reintroduce any sampling lower-bound language. |
| Ontic overclaim | Response-rank statements could be read as claims about hidden structure. | The reconstruction is explicitly up to simultaneous similarity on the registered response quotient; ledger items O3/O4 bound what is asserted; no ontic language anywhere. | Keep title, abstract, and outlook free of ontic vocabulary. |
| Single figure legibility | Four dense panels in one figure may not survive column width. | Panels rebuilt to one message each; family-normalized units give a single pass/fail line; an automated overlap gate plus manual pixel inspection guard the render; smallest fonts sit in the schematic panel only. | Re-run the figure script and the overlap gate after any caption or panel edit. |
| Single-author verifiability | One author, no lab: why trust the proofs and the runs? | Two independent mathematical audits are integrated and archived; all proofs are in the SM; every hardware artifact (jobs, calibration snapshots, manifests, hashes, scoring code) is public in release v1.0 and re-scorable end to end. | Keep the artifact release frozen and cited in the data-availability paragraph. |
