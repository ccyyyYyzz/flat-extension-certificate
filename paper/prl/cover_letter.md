# Cover letter — Physical Review Letters

Dear Editors,

We submit "Single-Slot Tomography Cannot Self-Certify Multitime Response Dimension" for consideration as a Letter.

**What is shown.** Informationally complete tomography of every single-time marginal can be provably blind to multitime response directions: we give a minimal two-qubit collision model in which a complete atomic tester family is exactly independent of a smooth, non-gauge memory rotation that one retained two-time correlator detects at first order. We then prove the positive counterpart: for a declared finite response order, a rank-saturated flat Hankel core uniquely determines every registered response at arbitrary depth, with explicit failure semantics — no order provenance means no certificate, a boundary made sharp by a delayed-chain impossibility family. A failure-aware perturbation theorem and a resource theorem (polynomial in the declared order versus the Theta(d^{4k}) affine dimension of unrestricted depth-k comb tomography) complete the package.

**Why it may interest a broad audience.** The result reframes a question every quantum platform faces — "when has a device been characterized completely enough?" — as a certifiable, falsifiable protocol property rather than an assumption. A preregistered, sealed blind benchmark on two IBM processors stress-tested the obstruction and reference-calibration branches of the audit on hardware we do not control: all planted hidden directions were detected with the frozen estimator, matched marginal-channel tomography detected none, and a preregistered follow-up identified a reproducible device-locked floor on one physical layout, consistent with readout bias; a preregistered device-referenced differential test on the same processor then detected all planted directions with zero null false positives.

**Scope discipline.** The Letter claims only what is proved: the certificate is relative to a declared intervention algebra and an externally supplied order bound; the structured-comparator tie and all open problems are stated explicitly in an eight-item ledger in the Supplemental Material.

The manuscript is a Letter with one figure and two short End Matter appendixes (body within the length limit); the Supplemental Material (21 pages) contains all proofs and the complete hardware provenance. Code and data are public at https://github.com/ccyyyYyzz/flat-extension-certificate (release v1.0). This work is not under consideration elsewhere.
